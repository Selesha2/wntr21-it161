Forms

Forms are an important part of your website as they allow for the user to interact with the page by sending data through that initiates the site in some way. There is a wide variety of different forms you can make that range from allowing the user to login to your website, email you questions they have, to scheduling an appointment for your business, and so much more. Basically, anywhere the user can input data is where a form tag starts. The reason they are so important is because they need to be usable. They need to be sufficient enough that anyone using your site can understand the questions it’s asking and that they do not get frustrated along the way and leave. When incorporating a form into your site avoid a lot of repetition and put time into creating the structure so that when you apply CSS, it will look appealing to the eye and “create more interesting, lively forms using the latest features.” (Kavinda, 2020) Simply adding a border and spacing around your login and password section or adding color and animation to a search or submit button will greatly improve the looks of your site. Which will also draw more attention and get more people to successfully use your site. As you come up with these different types of forms, you are going to have to think about the input type the user will be submitting. With HTML5, a dozen different types are available for you to use and it will be your job deciding which one is the most appropriate for your forms. “Users appreciate websites that automatically present number pads for entering phone numbers, or automatically advance fields as they enter them.” (LePage, 2019) Your goal when creating your site is to make the users experience as simple and easy as possible by going that extra step and thinking about their needs.
